MARY CHAPP Design & Illustration

Web and App Design

I wanted to get some practice at designing user interfaces for websites, but I also wanted to attempt a redesign of an existing company’s logo. Goodyear was celebrating its 100 year anniversary so it provided a fun challenge to redesign their logomark. Before starting, I did a lot of research on the history of the company’s logo. Turns out, the logo remained nearly the same since Goodyear first began its business. The color scheme was fine, although I did change the lemon yellow to a more goldenrod hue to reflect the level of value and trust the company has with its customers and product reliability. Many companies that are affiliated with the automotive industry use lines or wings to show progressive speed and moving forward. Even Goodyear’s original logo is slanted and has a winged foot for its mark. I kept that wing, but attached it to the “G” as a nod to their original winged foot. Their website at the time, required extensive redesigning. I redesigned their website with better navigation and added a mobile app for ordering tires on-the-go.
